Both are GLP-1 receptor agonists, but tirzepatide (the active ingredient in Zepbound® and Mounjaro®) acts on two receptors — GLP-1 and GIP — while semaglutide (Wegovy® and Ozempic®) targets only GLP-1.

That said, individual response varies, and semaglutide has a longer real-world safety track record. Our 90-Day Program uses tirzepatide as the default, but our physicians can help you decide which is the better fit for your body.

Side Effects & Safety

3 questions

The most common side effects include mild-to-moderate nausea, occasional constipation or diarrhea, fatigue, and mild injection site reactions during the first few weeks. Most side effects diminish significantly within 2–4 weeks as your body adjusts.

Our slow-titration protocol and dietitian guidance help minimize side effects. This is the main reason people quit GLP-1 therapy on their own — and it’s exactly where a supported program makes the biggest difference.

We use a comprehensive approach: gradual dose titration, personalized nutrition adjustments (smaller and more frequent meals, higher protein, adequate hydration), targeted supplements when needed, and lifestyle strategies to reduce discomfort.

Your dietitian is available for direct messaging to address specific issues as they come up. Active side-effect management is included in your program at no extra cost.

When prescribed and used properly, most peptides have excellent safety profiles based on the available clinical research. However, safety depends heavily on peptide selection, dosing, sourcing, and individual health factors.

All our peptides come from licensed U.S. pharmacies, are third-party tested, and are prescribed only after physician review. Never source peptides from unregulated online vendors — identity, purity, and potency cannot be verified.
