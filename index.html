The honest guide

GLP-1 weight loss, explained like a human.

Let’s slow down and talk about what’s actually happening in your body — and why this approach works when so many others quietly failed you. No jargon. No hype. Just the real picture, so you can decide with your eyes open.

What a GLP-1 actually does

GLP-1 stands for glucagon-like peptide-1. Your gut already makes it. After you eat, it tells your brain you’re full and tells your stomach to slow down. The problem? For many people, that signal runs weak. So hunger returns fast, cravings pile up, and the scale won’t budge no matter how hard you try.

GLP-1 medications like tirzepatide and semaglutide turn that signal back up. Suddenly the food noise gets quiet. You feel full sooner. You stay full longer. And for the first time in years, eating less doesn’t feel like a daily battle — it feels natural. That shift is the whole point. It’s not magic. It’s biology, finally working with you instead of against you.

Tirzepatide goes one step further. It hits two receptors instead of one — both GIP and GLP-1. That dual action is why it topped the field in trials, and it’s why we built our core program around it.

Why medication alone isn’t enough

Here’s where most companies stop. They ship a vial. They wish you luck. Then they disappear. And that gap is exactly where people struggle.

Because the medication solves appetite. But it doesn’t teach your body what to do with the calories it still needs. It doesn’t protect your muscle while the fat comes off. It doesn’t walk you through the nausea in week two, or the plateau in month three, or the question of what happens when you stop.

Those things need a human. Specifically, they need a registered dietitian who knows metabolic health cold. So while the vial does its job, we do ours — we make sure the weight you lose is fat, not muscle. We keep your protein high. We keep your energy up. And we keep you moving forward when motivation dips, because it always does at some point, and that’s normal.

Protecting your muscle is the whole game

This part matters more than almost anyone tells you. When you lose weight fast, some of it can come from muscle. And muscle is what keeps your metabolism strong, your body capable, and the weight from creeping back later.

In the SURMOUNT-1 trial, roughly three-quarters of the weight participants lost was fat mass. That’s a good ratio — but it doesn’t happen by accident. It happens when you eat enough protein and keep your muscles working. So our plan builds both in from day one. We’ll give you protein targets you can actually hit, and simple strength guidance that fits a real schedule. Not a boot camp. Just enough to keep what matters.

What results actually look like

Let’s be honest about the numbers, because you deserve honesty. In clinical trials, people on tirzepatide lost an average of 22.5% of their body weight over 72 weeks at the highest dose. On semaglutide, the average was about 15% over 68 weeks. Those are averages — some people lost more, some less. Your body, your dose, and your consistency all shape where you land.

The other truth: this takes time. The scale moves fastest in the early months, then settles into a steadier pace before reaching a plateau. That plateau isn’t failure — it’s your body finding a new normal. Our job is to help you get there safely, and to help you hold it once you do.

Side effects — the real talk

Most people do feel something in the first few weeks. Usually it’s mild nausea, maybe some fullness or a change in digestion. It tends to fade as your body adjusts, especially when the dose steps up slowly. That slow, careful titration is standard for a reason — it keeps side effects manageable.

Still, you shouldn’t have to guess your way through it. When something feels off, you message us. A real dietitian answers. We adjust the plan, tweak your meals, and help you ride it out. That support is the difference between quitting in week three and breaking through to week twelve.
